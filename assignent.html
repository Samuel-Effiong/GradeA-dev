Question 1 (10 marks)

Describe the major Native American civilizations that existed in America before the arrival of Europeans. Explain their way of life, culture, and contributions to early American society.

Marking Guide / Rubric:

Performance Level Marks Criteria

Good 8

Clear, accurate discussion covering several civilizations with appropriate supporting details
Fair 5

Basic description with some omissions or generalities
Poor 0

Incomplete or inaccurate response that misses key elements

Model Answer / Expected Response:

Native American civilizations before European contact were diverse across the continent. In the Eastern Woodlands, mound-building cultures such as the Hopewell and Mississippian built earthworks, developed long-distance trade networks, and organized complex social structures. In the Southwest, the Ancestral Puebloans (Anasazi) constructed multi-story pueblos and developed sophisticated irrigation to farm in arid environments. The Pacific Northwest supported rich cultures around abundant salmon and strong chiefs, with intricate art and long-standing trade relations. Plains groups, including various Sioux and Lakota communities, adapted to buffalo herds and seasonal migrations, maintaining strong oral traditions and communal governance. Collectively these peoples contributed to early American society through trade, exchange of ideas, architectural innovations, agricultural practices, and enduring cultural traditions that shaped the region long before European arrival.

Model Answer / Expected Response:

The American Revolution was sparked by a combination of political grievances (taxation without representation), economic restrictions, and the colonists’ emerging sense of identity and rights. The colonies declared independence in 1776 and, with French support, defeated Britain, establishing a republic based on liberty and rights. Its influence extended globally, inspiring other movements toward self-government and republican governance.

Model Answer / Expected Response:

Slavery shaped the American economy and politics for centuries, creating sectional tensions between the North and South. These conflicts contributed to the onset of the Civil War. The war’s major outcomes included the preservation of the Union, the abolition of slavery (emancipation), and legal amendments that redefined citizenship and rights. The nation reorganized around principles of equality and federal authority to enforce those rights.
